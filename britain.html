Making Tea

Tea is an integral part of British culture. Whether you are cold from the typical weather, crying over a breakup or ranting over an issue, tea is often the first step to solving any issue. Learning to make and serve tea properly is important, therefore.

Sarcasm

Sarcasm is part of the culture and not a personal dig. It is a means of expressing the complete opposite of what you are thinking/feeling for dramatic effect. Learning to take & serve it in your stride will enhance your character!

Personal Hygiene

The underground tube at rush hour is enough torture. Please don't add to this by not taking care of your body and personal hygiene prior to leaving your home!

Personal Conversations!

When on the train please be aware of other people. Yes, we know brexit is the major issue: we don't need an indirect lecture via your phone conversations! Keep it to a minimum.

God save the Queen!

The royal family is often a topic for conversations in the UK, however, keep any disparaging comments restricted to your inner circle for peace sake!

Crowded trains

This should be self explanitary; when in a crowded train/bus, do not occupy more than one seat. You will be the obejct of silent scorn should you not heed to this.
